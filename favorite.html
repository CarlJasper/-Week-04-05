    • 3 Idiots

    3 Idiots" is a 2009 Bollywood film directed by Rajkumar Hirani. It follows the story of three engineering students: Rancho, Farhan, and Raju, who navigate the pressures of academic life and their personal ambitions. The movie is known for its blend of comedy, drama, and social commentary.
    Rancho, played by Aamir Khan, is an unconventional and free-spirited student who challenges the traditional education system and encourages his friends to pursue their passions. Farhan, played by Madhavan, dreams of becoming a wildlife photographer but is pressured by his father to follow a conventional career path. Raju, played by Sharman Joshi, struggles with financial difficulties and familial expectations.
    The film alternates between their college days and a present-day quest to find Rancho, who has disappeared. Through a mix of flashbacks and current events, "3 Idiots" explores themes of friendship, the flaws in the education system, and the importance of following one's dreams. The movie was both a critical and commercial success, resonating with audiences for its humor, emotional depth, and inspirational message.

    Author

    3 Idiots" is based on the novel Five Point Someone by Chetan Bhagat. The book serves as the inspiration for the film's storyline, though the movie adapts and expands upon the original narrative. Chetan Bhagat is a well-known Indian author, and his works often explore themes related to contemporary Indian society and youth.

    Plot of the story

    3 Idiots" centers on the lives of three engineering students—Rancho, Farhan, and Raju—at a prestigious Indian college. The story alternates between their college days and the present, where the two friends are searching for their missing friend, Rancho.
    Through their journey to find Rancho, they reminisce about their college experiences, uncover hidden truths, and reflect on how Rancho's unconventional wisdom influenced their lives. The film highlights themes of friendship, the flaws in the education system, and the importance of following one's own path.

    Setting of the story

    The College Campus: The bulk of the story takes place at the Imperial College of Engineering (ICE), a prestigious but highly competitive institution in India. This setting showcases the intense pressure faced by students, the strict and traditional teaching methods enforced by the college dean, Virus, and the camaraderie and challenges experienced by the main characters. The campus is depicted as a high-pressure environment with a strong emphasis on academic excellence and conformity.
    The Present-Day Search: In the present timeline, the setting shifts to various locations as Farhan and Raju embark on a journey to find their missing friend, Rancho. This search takes them to different parts of India, highlighting contrasts between their past and present lives, and reflecting on their personal growth and the impact Rancho had on them.

    Characters of the story

    This are the Characters of the movie


    Rancho (Ranchoddas Shamaldas Chanchad)

    Played by Aamir Khan. Rancho is the free-spirited, brilliant, and unconventional student who challenges the traditional education system and inspires his friends to follow their passions.


    Farhan Qureshi

    Played by Madhavan. Farhan is one of Rancho’s close friends, passionate about wildlife photography but pressured by his father to pursue engineering.


    Raju Rastogi

    Played by Sharman Joshi. Raju is another of Rancho’s friends, struggling with financial difficulties and family expectations.


    Virus (Viru Sahastrabuddhe)

    Played by Boman Irani. Virus is the strict and authoritarian dean of the Imperial College of Engineering, representing the rigid and pressure-filled education system.


    Pia Sahastrabuddhe

    Played by Kareena Kapoor Khan. Pia is Virus’s daughter and a medical student who becomes romantically involved with Rancho.


    Chatur Ramalingam (Silencer)

    Played by Omi Vaidya. Chatur is a competitive and arrogant student who often contrasts with Rancho’s unconventional methods. He is driven by a desire to succeed and outshine others.
